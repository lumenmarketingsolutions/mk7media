What's Missing

One year in, two collections, and a story that hasn't reached anyone yet.

You told me this plainly on the call, and I want to name it back. What we have here is a distribution problem. The brand itself is already in the right place.

The woman who would pay several thousand dollars for a piece of jewelry that represents her journey exists. She is in Dubai, in Paris, in London, in New York, as well as in Lebanon. She has the money. She has the taste. She simply hasn't seen you yet.

What we build together is a way for your story to travel.

There are a few things on your site that are quietly getting in the way. The privacy popup on arrival breaks the spell before it begins. The story is buried behind a button most visitors won't click. The pieces are beautiful and still unnamed, which means a woman has nothing to hold onto once she leaves the page. A woman falls in love with a story she can carry.

These are small moves, and we fix them before we spend a dollar on ads.

ONEYour story arrives before your price tag.

A woman sees an ad. Cinematic. Quiet enough to make her stop scrolling. She clicks. The landing page continues the feeling she just had. By the time she sees a price, she has already decided she wants to live with the piece.

TWOEvery piece has a name and a story.

Each piece becomes a character of its own. A woman reborn, a kind of strength, a moment in a life. A buyer chooses the version of herself she wants to wear.

THREEA door for everyone, a door for the collector.

Some women want to click buy. Some want to speak with you and have something made. The system gives them both. The site becomes a gallery. WhatsApp becomes a private atelier. Everyone is welcomed through the door that fits them.

A note from me

I see a small hole in the collection that I'd offer to fill, carefully and only if you want it. A more accessible signature piece, or a small group of them. Something that carries the same story and the same craft as your higher work, at a price that gives a first-time buyer a way to begin. A tennis bracelet, a ring, a pendant, a pair of earrings. Whatever feels right inside your world, named and storied like everything else you do. It gives a new buyer a place to start, and it lets your higher pieces feel even more significant when she is ready for them. Made-to-order is fine. Nothing has to sit on a shelf.

This is an instinct I wanted to share, nothing more. Your collection is your art. If it doesn't feel right for the brand, we leave it alone and focus on what you already have.

The Videos

Treated as art in their own right.

Before the rest of the build, I want to talk about the videos directly. This is the piece I care about getting right more than anything else.

Picture a woman scrolling through her feed. Most of what passes is noise. Products. Captions. Offers. Then something appears that she cannot quite place. It moves slowly. The light is quiet. The camera lingers on a moment of a woman who is becoming something, and she has no idea what she is looking at yet. She stops. She watches. By the time the brand name arrives, the feeling has already arrived inside her.

That is what these videos will be. Pieces of film. Cinematic in the truest sense. Emotion-heavy. The kind of work that sits apart from the wall of content a woman has learned to tune out.

The closest reference I can point to for the vision I have here is the short film Ferrari just released around their new sailboat. Almost no dialogue. Almost no product shots. What carries the piece is feeling, and the feeling arrives a full minute before the logo does. That is the tier we are reaching for.

How the set comes together

Three pieces at launch. Two at the top of the funnel, each built as a first encounter with your world. Quiet, intentional, made to stop her mid-scroll and hold her there. I direct the creative personally. Video was the craft I loved before I loved marketing, eight years of it before this. This is the part of the work I am most excited to put into your hands.
